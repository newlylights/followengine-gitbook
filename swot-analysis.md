# SWOT Analysis for Competitors

**SWOT** (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) is the most accessible competitive analysis framework. Applied correctly to competitors, it reveals where to attack, defend, and differentiate.

## The SWOT Matrix

```
STRENGTHS                        WEAKNESSES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Internal factors the competitor   Internal factors that put them
executes well:                     at a disadvantage:
• Market position                 • Product gaps
• Technology advantage            • GTM weaknesses
• Brand equity                   • Talent gaps
• IP / patents                   • Customer satisfaction issues
• Distribution network           • Resource constraints
• Capital resources              • Technical debt

OPPORTUNITIES                    THREATS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
External factors they can         External factors that could
exploit for growth:               harm their position:
• Market expansion               • New entrants
• Technology shifts              • Regulatory changes
• Customer behavior changes      • Economic downturn
• Partnerships                   • Our strategic moves
• Acquisition targets            • Substitute products
```

## How to Conduct Competitor SWOT

### Step 1: Gather Intelligence

Collect data from:
- **Company website** — Product pages, pricing, case studies
- **Review platforms** — G2, TrustRadius, Capterra for product strengths/weaknesses
- **Social media** — Brand perception, customer complaints
- **Job postings** — Reveal investment areas and team gaps
- **Press releases / news** — Strategic moves, partnerships, funding
- **Earnings calls** — Public company priorities and challenges
- **LinkedIn** — Leadership changes, employee growth/loss

### Step 2: Distinguish Internal vs External

A common mistake is confusing SWOT dimensions:
- **Strengths & Weaknesses** = INTERNAL to the competitor (they control these)
- **Opportunities & Threats** = EXTERNAL (market forces, our actions)

### Step 3: Prioritize by Impact

Not all SWOT items are equal. Score each on:
- **Impact** (1-5): How much does this affect competitive dynamics?
- **Confidence** (1-5): How certain are you of this assessment?

### Step 4: Derive Strategic Implications

For each quadrant, ask: "So what do WE do about this?"

| SWOT Item | Our Response |
|-----------|-------------|
| Their strength in [X] | Avoid head-on competition; reframe buying criteria |
| Their weakness in [Y] | Exploit in messaging and product demos |
| Market opportunity [Z] | Can we capture it first? |
| Threat to them [W] | Accelerate this threat through our strategy |

## Example: SaaS Competitor SWOT

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----------|-----------|
| Largest market share (41%) | NPS score declining (-8 pts this year) |
| Strong brand recognition | Slow release cadence (1 major/quarter) |
| 2,000+ integration ecosystem | Enterprise onboarding takes 6+ weeks |
| Best-in-class API documentation | Mobile app rated 2.8★ |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|--------------|---------|
| SMB segment underserved by current pricing | Our new AI features surpass theirs |
| Growing demand for [category] | Regulatory changes may disrupt model |
| Partnership with [platform] | Open-source alternative gaining traction |
