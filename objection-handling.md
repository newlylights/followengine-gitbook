# Objection Handling

Competitive objection handling is where battle cards prove their value in live deals. This guide covers frameworks and examples.

## The FORCE Framework

For handling any competitive objection:

- **F** — **Acknowledge** the objection genuinely ("That makes sense...")
- **O** — **Reframe** the buying criteria ("What matters most for your team is...")
- **R** — **Respond** with proof ("Our customers typically see...")
- **C** — **Confirm** understanding ("Does that address your concern?")
- **E** — **Elevate** to a stronger position ("The real question is...")

## Common Objections & Responses

### "They're cheaper than you"

> ❌ **Don't say:** "We're worth it." (dismissive)
>
> ✅ **Say:** "Let's look at what's included. Our pricing includes [X, Y, Z] which [competitor] charges separately. When Acme switched, their total cost went down 30% because they eliminated [tools they were using alongside competitor]."

**Strategy:** Shift from price to Total Cost of Ownership (TCO). Include implementation, training, add-ons, and operational costs.

### "They're the market leader"

> ❌ **Don't say:** "But we're better at..." (sounds defensive)
>
> ✅ **Say:** "They've done a great job in this space. What we're hearing from customers who switched is that as [category] has evolved, the incumbents haven't kept pace with [specific innovation]. Let me show you what's different now."

**Strategy:** Acknowledge their position while framing innovation velocity as the new battleground.

### "We already use them"

> ❌ **Don't say:** "You should switch." (pressures buyer)
>
> ✅ **Say:** "Many of our customers came from [competitor]. The trigger was usually [specific pain point]. Is that something you've experienced, or are things working well?"

**Strategy:** Don't sell the switch — surface the pain that makes switching worthwhile.

### "They have [feature X] and you don't"

> ❌ **Don't say:** "We're working on it." (weak)
>
> ✅ **Say:** "Feature X is valuable for [specific use case]. What we've found is that most teams get more impact from [our compensating advantage] because [reason]. Feature X is on our H1 roadmap — here's what we're shipping in the meantime."

**Strategy:** Don't dismiss their feature. Elevate a different capability while being honest about your roadmap.

## Discovery Questions That Shift the Frame

The best objection handling happens before the objection is raised. These questions surface competitive weaknesses during discovery:

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| "What's one thing you wish [competitor] did better?" | Their pain points |
| "How does your team work around [competitor]'s limitations?" | Hidden costs/workarounds |
| "What would happen if [scenario competitor can't handle]?" | Risk awareness |
| "How has [competitor]'s support been during [event]?" | Service gaps |
| "What's changed about your needs since you chose [competitor]?" | Outgrown solution |

## Practice Makes Battle Cards

Battle cards deliver their ROI when reps practice objection handling — not just read it. Competitive roleplay sessions, using real recorded calls, improve objection handling effectiveness by **2-3x** compared to reading alone.
