# Win/Loss Analysis

**Win/Loss Analysis** is the systematic study of why deals are won or lost against specific competitors. It's the most direct link between competitive intelligence and revenue impact.

## Why Win/Loss Matters

According to [Forrester](https://www.forrester.com/), companies that systematically analyze competitive losses see **2-5% win rate improvement** within two quarters — directly attributable to CI-informed adjustments.

## The Win/Loss Process

```
Deal closes → Classification → Interview → Pattern analysis → Action
```

### 1. Classification

Every competitive deal gets tagged:
- **Competitor** involved
- **Win/Loss** outcome
- **Primary reason** (product, price, relationship, brand, implementation)
- **Deal size** and **sales cycle length**

### 2. Interviews

Within 2 weeks of close:
- **Buyer interviews** (conducted by neutral third party — not the sales rep)
- **Sales rep debrief** (internal perspective)
- **Sales engineer debrief** (technical perspective)

### 3. Pattern Analysis

After enough data points, patterns emerge:

| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Losing on price to Competitor A | They're discounting aggressively | Strengthen value messaging, ROI tools |
| Losing on features to Competitor B | They have genuine product advantage | Prioritize in roadmap, reframe buying criteria |
| Winning when our battle cards used | CI is working | Expand battle card coverage |
| Losing early-stage deals | Brand awareness gap | Increase top-of-funnel competitive content |

### 4. Action

Pattern analysis without action is wasted effort. Each finding gets:
- **Owner** (PM, PMM, Sales Enablement, etc.)
- **Action** (specific, time-bound)
- **Success metric** (how you'll know it worked)

## Connecting Win/Loss to CI Platforms

Modern CI platforms like [FollowEngine](https://followengine.com) close the loop between win/loss intelligence and proactive competitor monitoring:

1. Loss reason: "Competitor launched feature X" → FollowEngine alerts you to feature X's launch
2. Win reason: "Our AI capabilities" → FollowEngine tracks whether competitors are closing the AI gap
3. Pattern: "Losing to new entrant Y" → FollowEngine's Competitor Finder discovers similar threats
