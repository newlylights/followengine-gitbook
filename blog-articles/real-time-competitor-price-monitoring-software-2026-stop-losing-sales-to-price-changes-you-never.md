## Stop Tracking Prices. Start Reading Strategy.

Most pricing teams ask one question: "What's their price?"

The highest-performing teams ask a different question: "What's their pricing strategy — and what's the counter-move executed today?"

---

### 1. Pricing Strategy Shifts → Your Early Warning Radar

Patterns worth watching for:

- When competitors raise prices → it almost always precedes a feature launch. They're creating perceived value before adding real value. Counter-move: promote your existing feature set before their launch lands.
- When competitors discount aggressively → they're losing deals. Probably to you. Counter-move: hold pricing, emphasize value differentiation.
- When competitors introduce tiered pricing → they're segmenting their market. Counter-move: analyze which segment they're targeting and decide whether to compete or concede.

---

### 2. Funnel-Based Pricing Behavior → Where the Real Games Are Played

The most sophisticated competitors don't just set prices. They build pricing funnels:

- Landing page price ≠ checkout price? That's A/B testing on live customers. They're optimizing conversion at your expense.
- Prices personalized by traffic source, geography, or device? They're running advanced segmentation — and you should understand exactly how.
- Enterprise pricing hidden behind "Contact Sales"? They're moving upmarket and hiding the evidence. That's a strategic pivot you need to know about.

---

### 3. Market Positioning Signals → The Moves Before the Moves

Before a competitor announces a "strategic repositioning" — which they'll do in a press release 6 months after it starts — the pricing page tells you everything:

- Moving upmarket or downmarket? Bundling changes reveal it before the G2 category update.
- Product bundling shifts? That's not a product decision — it's a pricing strategy pivot. They're changing how they capture value.
- Feature shuffling between tiers? They're testing willingness-to-pay. This is the most honest market research you'll ever get from a competitor.

> 💡 The insight isn't the price point. It's the strategy. And FollowEngine decodes the difference automatically.
