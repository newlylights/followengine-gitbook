# Building Battle Cards

A **battle card** is a concise competitive reference that equips sales teams with the intelligence they need to handle competitive situations during customer conversations.

## Anatomy of a Great Battle Card

```
COMPETITOR BATTLE CARD: [Competitor Name]
Last Updated: [Date] | Owner: [PMM Team]

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT                              │
│    Quick facts: founded, funding, size, target   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 2. PRODUCT COMPARISON                            │
│    Feature-by-feature: ✓ us, ✗ them, why         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 3. STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES                        │
│    Their advantages → how to neutralize          │
│    Their gaps → questions to surface them        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 4. OBJECTION HANDLING                            │
│    Common objections + recommended responses     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 5. DISCOVERY QUESTIONS                           │
│    Questions that expose competitor weaknesses   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 6. WIN/LOSS DATA                                 │
│    Win rate vs this competitor, trends, reasons  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 7. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING                       │
│    One-sentence elevator pitch + proof points    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Battle Card Principles

### 1. One Page
If it doesn't fit on one page, it won't be used. Ruthlessly prioritize.

### 2. Sales Language
Not analyst-speak. Write how reps talk: "They'll say X. You say Y."

### 3. Actionable, Not Academic
Every section should answer: "What does the rep DO with this?"

### 4. Fresh Data
Stale battle cards are worse than none. Update at least quarterly.

### 5. Win/Loss Backed
Claims like "we're better at X" must be supported by actual win data.

## Objection Handling Framework

The most critical section. Structure every objection entry as:

| Objection | Response | Proof Point |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| "They're cheaper" | TCO comparison reframe | "Acme saved 40% on admin costs" |
| "They're the leader" | Innovation velocity argument | "We ship 3x more features/quarter" |
| "They have [feature]" | Roadmap insight + compensating advantage | "On our H1 roadmap, and we uniquely offer [Y]" |

## Discovery Questions That Expose Competitor Gaps

1. "How are you currently handling [pain point our competitor doesn't solve]?"
2. "What's your biggest frustration with your current [category] solution?"
3. "If you could change one thing about [competitor], what would it be?"
4. "How important is [our key differentiator] to your team's daily workflow?"
5. "What happens to your team when [gap scenario] occurs?"

## Keeping Battle Cards Updated
