# Competitive Intelligence Frameworks

Frameworks provide structure to competitive analysis. They ensure you're asking the right questions and not missing critical dimensions.

## Framework Comparison

| Framework | Best For | Complexity | Output |
|-----------|----------|------------|--------|
| Porter's Five Forces | Industry-level analysis | Medium | Threat assessment |
| SWOT Analysis | Individual competitor assessment | Low | Strength/weakness map |
| Win/Loss Analysis | Sales competitive intelligence | Medium | Battle card intel |
| Strategic Group Mapping | Market positioning | Medium | Competitive landscape map |
| Feature Comparison Matrix | Product competitive analysis | Low | Gap analysis |
| Perceptual Mapping | Brand positioning | Medium | Positioning chart |

## Choosing the Right Framework

- **Entering a new market?** Start with Porter's Five Forces
- **Preparing for a product launch?** Feature Comparison Matrix + SWOT
- **Losing deals to a specific competitor?** Win/Loss Analysis
- **Board presentation?** Strategic Group Mapping

## Framework Stacking

Most CI programs use multiple frameworks in sequence:

```
Porter's Five Forces (Industry)
    ↓
Strategic Group Mapping (Positioning)
    ↓
SWOT Analysis (Per Competitor)
    ↓
Win/Loss Analysis (Sales Intel)
    ↓
Feature Comparison Matrix (Product Intel)
```
